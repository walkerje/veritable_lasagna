# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

We want this project to be a welcoming, productive place to collaborate. We value:

- Respectful, constructive communication
- Patience and professionalism (especially during review)
- Empathy for different experience levels and backgrounds
- Clear technical disagreement without personal attacks
- Helping others learn and unblocking contributors when possible

## Unacceptable behavior

Examples of behavior that is not acceptable in project spaces include:

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, or persistent unwanted contact
- Discriminatory or demeaning remarks
- Personal attacks, insults, or hostile “pile-ons”
- Sexualized language or imagery
- Trolling, deliberate disruption, or repeated bad-faith arguing
- Publishing someone’s private information (doxxing) or encouraging others to do so
- Retaliation against anyone who reports a concern in good faith

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including (but not limited to):

- Issues, pull requests, code reviews, and comments
- Discussions, wikis, and other repository collaboration features
- Any community channels or events organized by the project

## How to report a concern

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct:

1. **If you feel unsafe**, step away and prioritize your safety first.
2. Report the situation to the maintainer(s) using one of these channels:
    - Email: **cragsman@bitshiftmountain.com** (recommended)
    - If email isn’t available, contact maintainer(s) privately via the hosting platform’s private reporting or security advisory features.

Please include, if you can:
- A brief description of what happened
- Links to relevant threads (issue/PR/discussion)
- Screenshots or quoted text (where appropriate)
- Dates/times and any context that may help

We will handle reports as discreetly as possible and share details only with those who need to act on them.

## Maintainer response and enforcement

maintainer(s) will make a good-faith effort to:
- Acknowledge receipt of a report within a reasonable timeframe
- Review available context and, when appropriate, ask for clarification
- Decide on an action that is proportionate to the situation

Possible actions include:
- A private reminder of expectations
- A request to stop specific behavior
- Comment moderation, content removal, or thread locking
- Temporary restrictions (e.g., limited participation)
- Permanent removal from project spaces (e.g., bans), for severe or repeated violations

Maintainer(s) may also act without a report when conduct issues are clear and immediate.

## Good-faith reports

We will not tolerate retaliation against people who raise concerns in good faith, even if the final decision is that no violation occurred.

## Appeals

If you believe an enforcement action was taken in error, you may contact the maintainer(s) privately with additional context. maintainer(s) will review the appeal and respond when practical. Appeals are not a guarantee of reversal.
